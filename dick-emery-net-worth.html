FAQs about Dick Emery

Who was Dick Emery?

Dick Emery was an English comedian and actor, known for his work on radio in the 1950s and his self-titled TV series The Dick Emery Show from 1963 to 1981​​.

What is Dick Emery famous for?

Emery is best remembered for his BBC TV show The Dick Emery Show, where he showcased his talent for impersonations across various characters, including Mandy the dumb blonde and Clarence, the outrageously camp man​​.

Did Dick Emery act in films?

Yes, he appeared in several films, including The Case of the Mukkinese Battle Horn (1956) and Yellow Submarine (1968), where he voiced several characters​​.

What were some of his catchphrases?

One of his most famous catchphrases, voiced by the character Mandy, was Ooh you are awful… but I like you!​​.

How did he contribute to radio and television?

Aside from his TV show, Emery contributed significantly to radio, appearing on shows like Workers’ Playtime and filling in for Harry Secombe in a 1957 episode of The Goon Show​​.

What was his personal life like?

Emery had a turbulent personal life, marrying five times and being involved in numerous affairs. He was also known for his hobbies, including flying, fast cars, and motorbikes​​​​.

How did Dick Emery die?

Emery passed away on 2 January 1983 from cardiorespiratory failure at the age of 67​​.

Did he have any children?

Yes, Emery had four children: Gilbert, Nicholas, Michael, and Eliza​​.

What were some of his hobbies?

He was an avid pilot, held a pilot’s license from 1961, and enjoyed flying, fast cars, and motorcycles​​​​.

What impact did he have on comedy?

His work influenced many comedians and shows that followed, including The Fast Show, Harry Enfield and Paul Whitehouse, and Little Britain. His characters, especially Mandy and Clarence, left a lasting impact on British comedy​​.
