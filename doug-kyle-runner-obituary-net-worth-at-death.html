Doug Kyle (runner) obituary &amp; net worth at death

Calgary, AB – The world of sports mourns the loss of Douglas Kyle, a revered Olympian and the visionary founder of the Calgary Marathon and Calgary Track and Field club. At the age of 91, Kyle’s journey concluded, leaving behind a trail of accomplishments and inspiration that continues to resonate with athletes and enthusiasts alike.

The news of Kyle’s passing shook the sports community as his family confirmed the tragic incident to CTV News. The fatal accident occurred in Calgary’s southeast, where a blue 2017 Chevrolet Spark – driven by Kyle – veered off the road at the intersection of 114 Avenue S.E. and 84 Street S.E.

Initial investigations revealed that the vehicle, traveling east on 114 Avenue towards 84 Street, approached a T-intersection and tragically lost control, resulting in the accident that claimed Kyle’s life.

While Kyle initially survived the accident and was transported to the hospital with serious but non-life-threatening injuries, his resilience ultimately gave way, and he passed away in the hospital.

The circumstances surrounding the accident continue to be under scrutiny, as police announced in a statement: “At this time, police continue to investigate the circumstances that led to the man’s death. Drugs or alcohol are not considered to be factors in this collision.”

A Family Grieves, a Community Mourns

The news of Kyle’s passing has left his family in a state of deep grief. His son, Robert Kyle, shared heartfelt sentiments: “We love him and we miss him… By the time we had arrived, he had already passed.” Reflecting on his grandfather’s legacy, James, Kyle’s grandson, expressed the extent of his dedication: “He was dedicated to the craft and he was dedicated to the city… his desire to make Calgary a better city for all.”

James also recalled shared experiences: “We used to do the CALTAF Father’s Day run, and the three of us every single year went out and jogged the whole way down… That had taken 30 years of my dad and I running and my son joining us.”

A Legacy Etched in History

Douglas Kyle’s legacy extended well beyond his Olympic achievements. Representing Canada at the 1956 and 1960 Olympic Games, his name became synonymous with dedication and achievement. His influence was palpable when he founded the Calgary Marathon in 1963, contributing to a thriving sporting community.

Jon Bird, interim executive director with Run Calgary and the host of the marathon, acknowledged the void left by Kyle’s passing: “Nineteen people started that race in 1963 and now we have over 10,000 people raising over a million dollars each year to charities, so his impact is pretty profound.”

The police’s call for witnesses or dash-cam footage remains in effect as the investigation into the accident continues.

Net Worth and Legacy

Douglas Kyle’s contributions to the world of sports extended far beyond his net worth. While his exact financial worth may be estimated between $1 million to $6 million, his true wealth lies in the inspiration he imparted, the lives he touched, and the enduring legacy he leaves behind.

The world of sports, particularly the running community, stands united in mourning the loss of an icon whose influence will continue to shape the community and inspire future generations.

Calgary police urge those with information related to the collision to come forward and assist in the ongoing investigation, upholding the memory of a sports legend whose legacy remains firmly rooted.
