Biography

Ken Kesey was born in La Junta, CO on September 17, 1935. American counterculture author best known for his novels One Flew Over the Cuckoo’s Nest, Sometimes a Great Notion, and Demon Box. He was a key member of the psychedelic literary collective known as the Merry Pranksters. The film adaptation of Kesey’s famous work, One Flew Over the Cuckoo’s Nest, led to an Oscar for actor Jack Nicholson. He was a champion wrestler in his high school and college days in Oregon; his Olympic dreams were crushed only by a serious shoulder injury. His other youthful hobbies included hypnotism and ventriloquism.
