FAQs About Justus Frantz

Who is Justus Frantz?

Justus Frantz is a celebrated German pianist, conductor, and television personality, known for his significant contributions to classical music and his role in founding the Philharmonie der Nationen and the Schleswig-Holstein Music Festival.

When and where was he born?

He was born on May 18, 1944, in Hohensalza, Germany (now Inowrocław, Poland).

What are some highlights of his career?

He has had an illustrious career, including performing with major orchestras like the Berlin Philharmonic and the New York Philharmonic under notable conductors such as Herbert von Karajan and Leonard Bernstein.

He also directed the Schleswig-Holstein Music Festival and hosted the television show Achtung! Klassik.

What is the Philharmonie der Nationen?

Founded by him in 1995, the Philharmonie der Nationen is an orchestra that aims to promote peace and unity through music, involving musicians from various nations.

Has he won any awards?

Among various honors, he was awarded the Golden Camera in 1993 for his work on the TV show Achtung! Klassik.
