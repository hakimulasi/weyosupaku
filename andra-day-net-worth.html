Andra Day Full Overview and Wiki

Early life and Education

Andra Day was born on December 30, 1984, in Edmonds, Washington, and grew up in Southeast San Diego. She started singing in church and taking dance lessons as a child.

Influenced by jazz icons, she attended Valencia Park Elementary and graduated from the San Diego School of Creative and Performing Arts in 2003. Day was inducted into Delta Sigma Theta sorority as an honorary member in 2019.

Career and Achievements

Andra’s career began unassumingly, with her voice capturing hearts via YouTube, a platform that has launched many contemporary artists.

She burst onto the scene with her debut album Cheers to the Fall in 2015, which was critically acclaimed and received a Grammy nomination for Best R&B Album.

Her powerful single Rise Up not only earned a Grammy nomination for Best R&B Performance but also became an inspirational anthem across America.

Musical Style and Genres

Day’s style is a blend of soul, R&B, blues, and jazz, influenced by iconic vocalists like Billie Holiday and Ella Fitzgerald.
